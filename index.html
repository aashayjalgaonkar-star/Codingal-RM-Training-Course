The RM Daily Rhythm — Login, Pipeline, Logout
ReadingQuiz
▼

Every day as an RM follows a clear structure. Understanding this rhythm is non-negotiable — the automated reports, the TL check-ins, and the sprint incentives all happen within this rhythm. Miss the rhythm, miss the results.

Your Daily Structure
  • Login Report (7:00 AM): Post your check-in time and shift time in #customer-relations. Then update your pipeline: Renewal enrolment, Renewal collection, High AR enrolment, High AR collection, RPTM scheduled, HPTM scheduled.
  • Morning Calling Block: Work through your lead buckets — Retargeting → High AR Unpaid → Future High AR → Renewal leads. Minimum 25–30 calls on Future High AR alone.
  • Mid-Day Automated Reports: The Codingal AI bot posts RM-wise performance reports around 1–3 PM: High AR Target vs Achieved, Renewal Enrolments, Renewal Revenue, M0 Cohort, PTM Report, Referral Program. React with the asked emoji to acknowledge.
  • Afternoon Sprint Window: Often the TL announces sprint incentives (e.g. "Get $500 = ₹350, Get $700+ = ₹550"). This is your high-focus calling block.
  • EOD DRR Update: Before logging out, post your DRR (Daily Revenue Run-rate), Actuals, and Expected in the channel when prompted by the TL.
  • Logout Report (4:00 PM): Post your checkout time + Count of dials (M0), Count of dials (M-1), # Renewal enrolments, # High AR enrolments, # Renewal collection, # High AR collection, # Referral/cross-sell generated.
  • 1:1 with TL: Sourav will check in with you before you log out — lead-by-lead review on your buckets. Come prepared.
⚠️
Don't be a "." RMPosting a single "." as your logout is noticed and flagged. Your TL reads these updates. A "." communicates nothing. Even if the day was tough, post actual numbers and a note.

Understanding Targets — MTD, DRR, and Pacing
ReadingQuiz

Key Metrics You'll See Every Day
TermWhat It MeansExample
MTDMonth-to-date — your cumulative achievement so far this monthMTD: 111 enrolments
TargetYour monthly goal for this metricTarget: 340 enrolments
Paced %Are you on track? (MTD ÷ Days elapsed × Working days)51% paced with 15/21 days done = slightly behind
DRRDaily Revenue Run-rate — how much you're averaging per dayDRR: 5.5 enrolments/day
Proj EOMProjected End of Month — where you'll land if current pace continuesProj EOM: ~172
Need/DayHow much you need to average per remaining working day to hit targetNeed/Day: $4,065
LMTDLast Month-to-Date — same period last month, for comparisonLMTD: 62 enrolments
🧮
How to read your standing in 10 secondsLook at Paced %. If it's below the % of working days elapsed, you're behind. Example: Day 15 of 21 = 71% of the month done. If you're only 51% paced, you have a gap. Need/Day tells you exactly what it takes to close it.

Marvin — Your Lead Management System
ReadingHands-on
▼

Marvin is Codingal's CRM — your source of truth for every lead you own. Everything you do with a lead — calls, messages, outcomes — must be logged in Marvin. If it's not in Marvin, it didn't happen.

Why Marvin Accuracy Is Non-Negotiable
  • The TL reviews Marvin lead-by-lead during your EOD 1:1
  • Leads get reshuffled if they're unactioned — and you lose them
  • The automated daily reports pull from Marvin data
  • Discount approvals require data that must match Marvin
  • Google Sheets and Marvin must always be in sync — update both
What to Log After Every Call
  • Call outcome (answered, not answered, callback scheduled)
  • Parent's sentiment and any objections raised
  • Next action and follow-up date
  • Lead stage update (e.g. Renewal Enrolment → Renewal Collection)
  • Any discount or payment link generated
⚠️
Duplicate lead alertIf you find a lead assigned to two RMs in Marvin (e.g. one in Marvin + another in the target sheet), immediately flag it in #customer-relations with the student ID and both RM names so the TL can resolve it. Do not work both independently.

What is a Renewal — and When to Initiate It
Reading
▼

A renewal is when an existing Codingal student continues their learning journey by purchasing their next course package. This is the single most important transaction you're responsible for.

⏰
When to initiateA renewal is initiated when the student has 8 classes remaining in their current course. This ensures smooth continuation, zero pressure on parents, and enough time for the RM to share progress and address concerns before the student runs out of classes.
Your Role During the Renewal
  • Track upcoming renewals on your dashboard
  • Conduct a structured and positive Renewal PTM (parent-teacher-manager meeting)
  • Share data: class reports, teacher notes, student strengths
  • Recommend the best learning path (next course/level)
  • Guide the parent to complete the renewal smoothly
  • Record and follow up on payment status in Marvin and sheets

Credit thresholds for auto-discontinuationNon-auto-debit: at –2 credits. Auto-debit via Stripe: at –4 credits (failed payments). Auto-debit via Razorpay: at –6 credits.
Reactivation Pitch Styles

The Renewal Conversation Framework — Rapport → Value → Course → Close
ReadingRoleplay
▼
Step 1 — Rapport (First 60 seconds)
"Hi [Parent Name], this is [Your Name] from Codingal! I hope [child's name] is doing well. I was just looking at their class reports and I wanted to share some great progress with you — do you have 5 minutes?"
Step 2 — Value Showcase (Progress Report)
"[Child's name] has completed [X] classes and [teacher name] mentioned that they've really improved in [specific area]. They're now working on [module/project]. Their consistency has been excellent — [X out of Y] classes attended."
  • Always mention something specific — a project, a module, a teacher comment
  • Generic praise ("doing well") is weak. Specific praise builds trust
  • Connect progress to the parent's original goal when they enrolled
Step 3 — Course Recommendation
"Since [child] has completed [current course], the natural next step for them is [next course] — this is designed for kids at exactly this level and it builds directly on what they've already learned. In this course they'll learn [2-3 specific things]."

Use the Course Suggester (v0-course-suggester-tool.vercel.app/index.html) to identify the exact right next course before the call.

Step 4 — Close
"To keep [child]'s momentum going without any break, I'd recommend we get the renewal done today so [teacher name] can immediately plan the next set of classes. I can share the payment link now — which works better for you, full payment or an instalment option?"
🔑
The close always offers two options — not a yes/no"Full payment or instalment?" vs "Would you like to renew?" The first assumes they will. The second opens the door to "no."
Non-Responding Parents — Teacher Outreach Protocol
  • Call the parent (1st attempt)
  • Send a WhatsApp message with the progress update
  • If no response in 24 hours, message the student's teacher asking them to check with the parent
  • Log every attempt in Marvin with timestamps

📌
Use this as your quick reference before every renewal action. Wrong status or wrong class action creates operational problems downstream — for the student, the teacher, and the batch. When in doubt, check this guide first.
🧑‍🎓 Student Status — Which One to Use?

There are three statuses you'll use as an RM. Each has a specific meaning — don't guess, always apply the right one based on the situation below.

StatusWhen to ApplyWhat It Means in Simple Terms
Active Curriculum change · Teacher change · Time change · Batch change Student is continuing normally — only internal adjustments are happening. Nothing changes from the student's perspective.
Paused Parent requests a break OR wants to stop but still has positive credits Student is temporarily not attending but will likely come back. Credits are preserved.
Discontinued Credits are zero or negative AND student is not continuing Student has permanently stopped. No more classes expected.
⚠️
Common mistake: Marking a student as Discontinued when they still have credits. If credits exist → always use Paused, never Discontinued. Discontinued is only for zero/negative credit situations.
🔄 Detailed Status Logic (Internal)

Use this for edge cases where the simple guide above isn't enough.

StatusExact Scenario
Active Class deleted for operational reasons only — batch change, teacher change, onboarding adjustments. Student is still enrolled and attending.
Paused Student has positive credits but is not taking classes temporarily. Parent may resume anytime.
Discontinued Any of the following: Credits fully used (0 or negative) · Parent refuses to continue · No payment for upcoming classes · Refund taken after attending classes
🧩 Batch & Class Management — The Golden Rules

This is where most mistakes happen. Follow these rules exactly — the action depends on how many students are in the batch.

ActionWhen to UseCritical Rule
Delete Course Class Only when 1 student is left in the batch ❗ If more than 1 student → DO NOT delete the class
Remove Student from Batch When student shifts (Plus → Prime), batch change, or teacher change Use this instead of deleting when multiple students are in the batch
💡
Quick rule to remember: Only 1 student in batch? → Delete class. More than 1? → Remove the student, leave the class intact for the others.

Your Response"Speaking English and speaking confidently in front of others are actually very different skills. This course focuses on clarity, structure, delivery, and confidence — not just language. Even children who are fluent often struggle with presentations, debates, and thinking on their feet."
❓ "Let me think about it"
Your Response"Absolutely — that makes sense. Since [child] has only 8 classes left, what I'd suggest is we schedule a final call for [specific date] so there's no gap in classes while you decide. Does [time] work for you?"
Always anchor a follow-up date. "Think about it" without a follow-up date = lost deal.
❓ "The price is too high"
Your Response"I hear you — and I want to make sure [child] can continue without any break. Let me check what flexible options I can offer. We have instalment plans that split the cost across 2–3 months. Can I run you through what that would look like?"

Your Response"Completely valid. This course actually helps them perform better in everything else — when a child communicates clearly, effort in other areas naturally reduces. And we can adjust the frequency to just 1 class per week if that helps."

Your Response"Schools focus on curriculum completion. We focus on individual practice, personalised feedback, and building the specific skill of confident expression — which schools simply don't have the bandwidth to do. Our teacher-to-student ratio and feedback loop is very different."
❓ "Will this really show results?"
Your Response"Parents typically notice more confidence in conversations, better class participation, and clearer expression of thoughts within the first few weeks. The structure of consistent practice + feedback is what drives results — it's not motivation-based."
